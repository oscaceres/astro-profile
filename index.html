About Me

I am a rising senior at the University of California, Los Angeles studying astrophysics. I am interested in pursuing a Ph.D. in Physics and/or Astronomy. My primary interests are in instrumentation, galaxy formation and evolution, and exoplanets.

Research

Undergraduate Research Assistant for Dr. R Michael Rich

Over 500 hours of remote operation of the Centurion 28-inch and the Centurion 18-inch telescopes. I assisted with the Shane 3-meter Telescope and the Kast Double Spectrograph by learning how to take arcs, dome flats, biases, and spectra of targets and communicating with the telescope operators and support astronomers.

McNair Scholars Program

I presented at UCLA's Undergraduate Research Week on Imaging Low Surface Brightness Galaxies in Hydrogen-Alpha Emission. As part of my participation in the program, I am attending seminars, workshops, and the Summer Research Institute to prepare for graduate school and learn how to conduct an independent research project.

Intro-to-Astro-2021 Summer Workshop

Over 8 weeks in the summer of 2021, I attended weekly lectures to learn more about the role of Python in astronomy. I developed a basic understanding of various modules including pandas, matplotlib, and numpy. The goal of the course is to prepare students for a paid internship in astronomy research.
